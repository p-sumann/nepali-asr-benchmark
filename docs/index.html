Abstract

Multilingual pretrained models nominally support Nepali, yet no controlled benchmark has compared them under a single fine-tuning protocol. We fine-tune six pretrained models (XLSR-53, IndicWav2Vec, MMS-1B, Whisper-Medium, Whisper-Large-v3-Turbo, and Conformer-Hi) spanning CTC self-supervised, autoregressive encoder-decoder, and hybrid Conformer-CTC architectures, on the OpenSLR SLR54 Nepali corpus (~165 hours) using identical preprocessing, splits, optimizer, and family-matched learning-rate schedules. We evaluate Word Error Rate (WER), Character Error Rate (CER), and Real-Time Factor (RTF) on three independent test sets (OpenSLR, FLEURS, Common Voice). Whisper-Large-v3-Turbo (14.76% WER) and IndicWav2Vec (14.89% WER) tie at the top despite a 9× parameter gap and 40× pretraining-data gap, providing direct empirical evidence that language-family proximity in pretraining can substitute for raw scale for in-domain Nepali. CTC decoders run up to 29× faster than autoregressive Whisper at the same accuracy, flipping the practical deployment preference toward CTC under any latency budget. Massively multilingual pretraining (MMS-1B) yields the smallest out-of-domain degradation on FLEURS (+12.55 pp), indicating that scale buys robustness rather than peak in-domain accuracy. The resulting benchmark provides the first standardized, multi-model, efficiency-aware reference numbers for Nepali ASR.

Key Findings

1. Language-family proximity beats raw scale. IndicWav2Vec (94 M params, pretrained on Indic languages) ties Whisper-Large-v3-Turbo (809 M params) on in-domain Nepali WER (14.89% vs 14.76%). A 9× parameter gap and a 40× pretraining-data gap collapse when the pretraining corpus actually speaks your language family.
2. CTC decoders are 29× faster than autoregressive Whisper at the same accuracy. For latency-sensitive Nepali ASR deployment (live captioning, IVR, on-device), CTC is the default architecture choice.
3. Massive multilingual pretraining buys robustness, not peak accuracy. MMS-1B has the worst in-domain WER of the six but the smallest degradation on out-of-domain FLEURS (+12.55 pp). Scale isn't accuracy; scale is generalization.

🤗 Six fine-tuned Nepali ASR checkpoints

Each ships with a usable inference snippet (5 lines) and full configuration. Download once, transcribe locally.

  • sumanpaudel1997/nepali-asr-whisper-turbo (best in-domain, 14.76% WER)
  • sumanpaudel1997/nepali-asr-indicwav2vec (ties top with 9× fewer params)
  • sumanpaudel1997/nepali-asr-whisper-medium
  • sumanpaudel1997/nepali-asr-xlsr-53
